# Can AI Draw the Soul? A Reflexive Diagram of the Soul

# Abstract

This article introduces the Reflexive Diagram of the Soul (RDS), a theoretical framework that reconsiders artificial intelligence as a medium for revealing, rather than reproducing, human interiority. Building upon a Three-Layer and Two-Stream Model (TTM) integrating embodied perception, algorithmic mediation, and symbolic interpretation, the study develops RDS through three author-led practice-based projects: an AI-assisted digital funeral system, an affect-responsive home companion device, and a biosignal-driven symbolic visualization platform. The paper argues that AI functions as a recursive mirror that makes visible the boundary between the computable and the incomputable. Rather than drawing the soul itself, AI traces its relational contours through feedback, symbolization, and reflection. The framework contributes a new ontological perspective on human–AI relations by positioning AI as a reflective mediator within processes of sensation, affect, memory, symbolization, and ethical self-understanding.

# Contribution

- Proposes the Reflexive Diagram of the Soul (RDS), a novel theoretical model that conceptualizes AI as a reflective medium revealing the relational structure of human interiority through recursive cycles of sensation, feedback, symbolization, and reflection.
    
- Develops the Three-Layer and Two-Stream Model (TTM), integrating phenomenology, post-phenomenology, affective computing, symbolic systems, and design ethics into a unified framework for understanding human–AI mediation.
    
- Demonstrates, through three practice-based research projects, how AI can facilitate self-reflection and symbolic meaning-making while preserving the distinction between computable processes and incomputable dimensions of human experience.
