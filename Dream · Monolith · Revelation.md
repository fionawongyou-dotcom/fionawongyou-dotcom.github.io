# Dream · Monolith · Revelation

# Exhibition Statement

*Dream · Monolith · Revelation* explores humanity's enduring desire to transcend earthly boundaries and imagine life beyond the known world. Taking as its point of departure the ancient Chinese legend of *Riding the Raft in the Eighth Month* (八月乘槎) recorded in Zhang Hua's *Bowuzhi* (*Records of the Investigation of Things*), the exhibition traces a continuum of cosmic imagination extending from mythological narratives to contemporary space exploration. 

The story of the celestial raft presents one of the earliest Chinese visions of space travel. Although framed as a myth, it reflects a profound aspiration to break through the limits of the known world and reach unfamiliar realms. More than seventeen centuries later, humanity has transformed this ancient dream into technological reality. Yet the desire underlying both remains unchanged: the search for a harmonious homeland beyond present horizons. 

Extending this historical narrative, the exhibition reconstructs an “ordinary” space habitat through an interdisciplinary framework that brings together artists, scientists, engineers, architects, designers, and researchers. Rather than presenting outer space as a distant frontier reserved for scientific exploration or geopolitical competition, the exhibition asks how space might become a livable environment and a shared cultural imagination for ordinary people. 

The exhibition unfolds through three conceptual movements: *Dream*, *Monolith*, and *Revelation*. Visitors travel through three chapters—*Departure with a Dream*, *Place with a Monolith*, and *Return with a Revelation*—forming an embodied journey across time, space, mythology, and future imaginaries. The exhibition proposes Space Art not merely as a technologically driven practice, but as a humanistic exploration rooted in embodiment, cosmological imagination, and humanity's shared longing for the cosmos.

Folding together the raft of the Western Jin Dynasty and the spacecraft of the present, *Dream · Monolith · Revelation* seeks a resonance across time and space. Through artworks, scientific research, speculative design, aerospace technologies, and cultural narratives, the exhibition creates a dialogue between ancient cosmologies and future civilizations, connecting humanity's earliest dreams of the stars with contemporary visions of life beyond Earth.
