# Byte of the Mustard Seed Garden

## Exhibition Statement

*Byte of the Mustard Seed Garden* is a solo exhibition by artist Hu Shasha that explores the encounter between traditional Chinese aesthetic systems and contemporary digital culture.

Borrowing its title from *The Mustard Seed Garden Manual of Painting*—one of the most influential painting manuals in Chinese art history—the exhibition examines how visual knowledge, artistic memory, and cultural transmission transform when translated into digital environments. Through painting and mixed-media practices, Hu Shasha reinterprets classical landscape traditions, calligraphic gestures, and organic forms through a contemporary visual language shaped by screens, data flows, and fragmented perception.

The exhibition proposes a dialogue between the “mustard seed,” a metaphor for the microcosm in classical Chinese thought, and the “byte,” the fundamental unit of digital information. Within this tension between tradition and technology, nature and computation, memory and simulation, Hu constructs an imagined landscape where historical aesthetics continue to evolve rather than remain fixed in the past.

Presented within The Cure Room, the exhibition invites audiences to navigate a hybrid visual ecology in which digital culture does not erase traditional knowledge but becomes a new medium through which cultural memory can be reactivated and transformed.

### Curatorial Focus

The exhibition formed part of The Cure Room's experimental exhibition program investigating emerging relationships between contemporary art, technology, cultural memory, and new forms of perception. Through Hu Shasha's practice, the project examined how traditional visual systems may continue to generate new meanings within digitally mediated environments.
