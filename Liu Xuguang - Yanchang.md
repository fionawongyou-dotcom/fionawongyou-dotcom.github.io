# Liu Xuguang: Yanchang

# Exhibition Statement

_Yanchang_ (Diffraction Field) is a solo exhibition by Chinese artist Liu Xuguang that explores the relationships between cosmology, consciousness, history, and contemporary existence. Drawing inspiration from the concept of _Da Yan_ (Great Expansion) in the _Yijing_ (_Book of Changes_), the exhibition investigates processes of transformation, generation, and emergence that shape both the universe and human experience.

Central to the exhibition is the recurring visual motif of the character _Bu_ (卜), which first appeared in Liu Xuguang's 1993 work _Heaven and Earth_. Originating from the crack patterns left on oracle bones, the symbol represents traces of interaction between humanity and the cosmos. Throughout the artist's practice, this element has evolved into a visual language through which questions of multidimensional space, perception, and existence are explored.

For Liu Xuguang, nature serves as the origin of thought and imagination. Rooted in the historical and cultural landscapes of the Yellow River civilization, his works investigate the relationship between macrocosmic structures and subjective consciousness, between historical memory and contemporary experience. Through painting, symbolic systems, and spatial compositions, the artist seeks to reveal the mechanisms through which meaning, perception, and reality emerge.

The exhibition also presents works such as _Dialogue with Voltaire_ and _Dialogue with Walter Benjamin_, extending these investigations into philosophical territory. Across the exhibition, Liu constructs a field where subjective and objective realities, time and space, image and consciousness coexist. This "Yanchang" becomes a generative space between presence and absence, echoing Eastern philosophical inquiries into emptiness, transformation, and becoming.
