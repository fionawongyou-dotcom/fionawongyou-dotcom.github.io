# The Flow

# Exhibition Statement

*The Flow* is the first solo exhibition by Chinese artist Li Yuyu. Through painting, sculpture, and installation, the exhibition examines the relationship between personal memory, regional culture, consumerism, and the formation of subjectivity within the context of China's Reform and Opening-Up era.

As both a Hakka and Chaoshan descendant growing up in Guangdong's coastal region, Li Yuyu's life trajectory mirrors the social transformation of southern China during the 1980s and 1990s. His artistic practice draws from the everyday visual and material culture of this period, translating collective memories into a contemporary artistic language. Through references to popular culture, local cuisine, commercial imagery, and vernacular aesthetics, the artist constructs a portrait of a generation shaped by economic reform, globalization, and emerging consumer culture.

Objects such as roast pig, preserved meat, canned fish, commercial packaging, and mass-produced commodities appear repeatedly throughout the exhibition. Removed from their original contexts and transformed through artistic intervention, these familiar objects become carriers of memory, desire, nostalgia, and cultural identity. Beneath their playful and colorful appearance lies a reflection on the social changes that reshaped everyday life in southern China.

The exhibition title, *The Flow*, is borrowed from the classic Cantonese song of the same name performed by Paula Tsui. Its lyrics evoke a generation's longing for freedom, mobility, and self-determination. Within the exhibition, “flow” becomes both a metaphor for historical transformation and an expression of individual subjectivity navigating the currents of contemporary society.

Through humor, affection, and critical observation, *The Flow* explores how personal histories intersect with broader social narratives, transforming ordinary cultural experiences into reflections on memory, identity, and the spirit of an era.
