# Electron Human Transition

# Exhibition Statement

*Electron Human Transition* is a solo project by artist Tao Duhan that explores the evolving relationship between the human body, scientific knowledge, technological systems, and the future of human existence.

Borrowing visual language from medical imaging, scientific observation, electronic infrastructures, and technological interfaces, the exhibition examines the human body as both a biological organism and a continuously transforming information system. Skeletons, X-ray imagery, data structures, and technological diagrams become tools for investigating how contemporary technologies reshape human perception, identity, and agency.

The concept of "transition" refers not only to technological advancement but also to a broader transformation of human subjectivity. As scientific systems increasingly mediate how we observe, measure, and understand ourselves, the boundaries between body and machine, organic and synthetic, individual and network become increasingly fluid. The exhibition asks what remains fundamentally human within a reality shaped by technological acceleration.

Presented within The Cure Room, the project transforms the exhibition space into a speculative environment where scientific visualization intersects with personal reflection. Rather than celebrating technology uncritically, the works invite viewers to consider how systems of observation, control, and knowledge production participate in constructing contemporary experience.

Through installations, images, and spatial interventions, *Electron Human Transition* reflects on the uncertain passage between biological existence and technological futures, exploring how humanity continuously negotiates its position within increasingly complex technological environments.
