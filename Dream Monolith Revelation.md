# Dream · Monolith · Revelation

# Artwork Statement

_Dream · Monolith · Revelation_ is an experimental film that began with a recurring dream from my childhood: again and again, I dreamed of flying. Long before I encountered astronomy, contemporary art, or space technology, these dreams shaped my imagination of the unknown and planted an enduring desire to transcend the limits of the earth.

As I grew older, this personal memory became intertwined with a much older cultural narrative—the legend of _Riding the Raft in the Eighth Month_ (八月乘槎) recorded in Zhang Hua’s _Bowuzhi_ (_Records of Investigation into Things_). In the tale, a traveler follows a celestial raft across the Milky Way and reaches the realm of the stars. The story presents one of the earliest Chinese imaginations of cosmic travel and offers a mythological counterpart to contemporary space exploration.

The film unfolds through three interconnected movements—Dream, Monolith, and Revelation. Dream represents the primordial human longing to reach beyond the visible world. Monolith symbolizes the technological systems, scientific infrastructures, and cultural monuments through which humanity attempts to materialize that longing. Revelation emerges in the encounter between imagination and reality, where new understandings of ourselves and the cosmos become possible.

Created alongside the exhibition of the same title, the project invited artists from eight countries to reinterpret and reconstruct the narrative of _Riding the Raft in the Eighth Month_ from diverse cultural perspectives. Through moving images, mythology, archival materials, and contemporary visions of spaceflight, the work establishes a dialogue between personal memory, ancient cosmology, and modern aerospace technologies.

Ultimately, _Dream · Monolith · Revelation_ is both a search and a response. It is an attempt to recover the recurring dream that accompanied me throughout childhood and to understand why humanity continues to look toward the stars. Through the journey from dream to monument and from monument to revelation, the work reflects on the relationship between imagination, technology, and the enduring human desire to transcend our earthly condition.
