# The Evolution of Space Art: Dream, Monolith and Revelation


# Abstract

This article traces the historical development of Space Art from prehistoric stargazing and cosmological imagination to contemporary artistic practices associated with space exploration, satellite technologies, and cultural reflection. Rather than presenting a linear chronology, the paper proposes three interpretive axes—Dream, Monolith, and Revelation—to examine how Space Art has evolved alongside scientific advancement, political ideology, technological infrastructures, and changing understandings of humanity’s relationship to the cosmos.

# Contribution

- Proposes a historical framework for understanding the evolution of Space Art through the concepts of Dream, Monolith, and Revelation.
    
- Establishes Space Art as an interdisciplinary field linking artistic practice, scientific knowledge, technological development, and cultural theory.
    
- Introduces Cosmotechnics as a framework for understanding culturally diverse futures of Space Art beyond dominant Space Race narratives.
