# Qiwu: Adjustment of Controversies
![](https://p.ipic.vip/rj9w6o.jpg)
# Abstract

This paper presents _Qiwu: Adjustment of Controversies_, an interactive media artwork that establishes a decentralized computational ecology integrating astronomical data, human neurophysiology, and artificial intelligence. Drawing upon Zhuangzi’s philosophy of _Qiwu_ (the equalization of things), the system dissolves hierarchical distinctions between cosmic processes, embodied cognition, and algorithmic mediation. Through a continuous data-metabolic pipeline combining real-time Solar Dynamics Observatory (SDO) telemetry, frontal EEG signals, false-color cartography, implicit neural representations, and AI-generated poetic language, the work produces evolving visual and textual landscapes. The project proposes a non-cybernetic model of human–machine–cosmos interaction, exploring contemporary relationships among consciousness, technology, and nature through immersive computational media.

# Contribution

- Proposes a decentralized interactive framework grounded in Zhuangzi’s concept of _Qiwu_, establishing a non-hierarchical relationship among cosmic telemetry, human consciousness, and artificial intelligence within a unified computational ecology.
    
- Develops a multidirectional data-metabolic pipeline that fuses real-time solar observation data and EEG phase transitions through implicit neural representations, false-color visualization, and AI-assisted semantic generation.
    
- Introduces a cross-scale aesthetic methodology that transforms scientific imaging techniques and astronomical data into immersive computational artworks, advancing human–machine–cosmos symbiosis in interactive media art.
