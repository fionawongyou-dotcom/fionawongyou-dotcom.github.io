# Use Subjectivity as a Curatorial Method in Space Art
![](https://p.ipic.vip/ydhenm.jpg)
# Abstract

This paper explores subjectivity as a curatorial method within Space Art exhibitions. Based on the author's curatorial practice in _Dream · Monolith · Revelation_, the study investigates how personal dreams, bodily perception, cultural identity, and phenomenological experience can function as active curatorial frameworks. Rather than treating curatorship as a neutral organizational activity, the paper argues that curatorial subjectivity shapes exhibition narratives, artwork selection, spatial design, and audience engagement. Through discussions of embodiment, dream experience, and cosmological imagination, the research examines how curatorial practice can mediate relationships among artists, artworks, audiences, and the broader cultural understanding of outer space.

# Contribution

- Proposes subjectivity as a curatorial methodology in Space Art, positioning personal dreams, embodied experiences, and cultural memory as active components of exhibition-making.
    
- Develops a phenomenological framework linking bodily perception, cosmological imagination, and curatorial practice, demonstrating how exhibitions can function as embodied narrative environments.
    
- Examines audience meaning-making through embodiment and environmental interaction, extending discussions of curatorial agency in technology-based art exhibitions.
