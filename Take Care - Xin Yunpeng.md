# Take Care

## Exhibition Statement

*Take Care* was the first solo exhibition by artist Xin Yunpeng, presenting a new body of installation works developed specifically for the exhibition. Conceived within a remarkably short preparation period, the project brought together a series of newly realized works that explored relationships between affection, communication, participation, and everyday experience. 

The exhibition title, *Take Care* (慢走), originates from a common Chinese farewell phrase. While seemingly ordinary and polite, the expression contains multiple layers of meaning—parting, concern, distance, companionship, and uncertainty. Rather than treating the phrase as a fixed concept, the exhibition transformed it into an open framework through which viewers could construct their own interpretations. As the artist remarked, the title was not something to be explained but something to be carried further by others. 

Throughout the exhibition, Xin Yunpeng approached art through humor, ambiguity, and participation. His works often appeared playful on the surface, yet beneath their accessibility lay carefully constructed situations that invited reflection on human relationships and systems of meaning. The artist described this strategy as a way of attracting audiences into a work before confronting them with more complex questions. 

One of the central works, *If I Say I Love You Again, You Don't Want to Hear It*, offered viewers multiple interpretive paths rather than a single narrative. The artist compared himself not to a conspirator but to an “Eros,” proposing artworks as arrows that create encounters between people, emotions, and imagination. The exhibition therefore positioned love, communication, misunderstanding, and participation as active forces within artistic experience. 

Rather than treating visitors as passive spectators, *Take Care* invited audiences to become participants within an evolving situation. For Xin, the exhibition was less about displaying finished objects than about creating conditions in which viewers, artworks, and space could jointly produce meaning. As he suggested, contemporary exhibitions become meaningful when people “make it together.”

## Related Programs

### Opening Exhibition of the Chunchun Contemporary Art Space Annual Program

As the first exhibition of Chunchun Contemporary Art Space's annual curatorial program, *Take Care* introduced a series dedicated to presenting emerging forms of contemporary Chinese art and supporting young artists working across installation, sculpture, painting, and interdisciplinary practices. 

### Artist Interview: "Take Care, Eros"

A published dialogue between curator Fiona You WANG and artist Xin Yunpeng explored themes of artistic production, participation, love, authorship, and contemporary exhibition-making. The interview became an important companion text to the exhibition.
