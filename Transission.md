# Transission

# Artwork Statement

_Transission_ is a Cross-Species Listening Installation Based on Real-Time Sensing and Intelligent Media.

This installation captures a diverse array of signals emitted by the earth and translates them instantaneously into rhythmic soundscapes, enabling humans to “hear” the breath of nature and the pulse of the planet. Centered on a seven-meter-tall conical tent, its form echoes the communal shelter structures of African tribes, symbolizing the symbiotic relationship between humans, nature and communities. The tent also functions as a colossal earpiece, wrapping audiences in a sonic field that lulls them into a primal, serene cycle of perception.

Powered by solar lighting and constructed from renewable materials, the work embraces an ESG philosophy that harmonizes environmental sustainability with urban public accessibility. Meanwhile, its open and transparent sensing mechanism demystifies technology—instead of being an opaque black box, it becomes an understandable, shareable medium bridging humans and the ecosystem.

In the gentlest of tones, the installation poses a profound question: When technology paves the way to the future, what exactly do we arrive at? The answer is connection, and symbiosis.
