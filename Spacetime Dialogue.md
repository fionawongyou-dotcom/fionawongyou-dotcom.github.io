# Spacetime Dialogue: Integrating Astronomical Data and Khoomei in Spatial Installation

# Abstract

This paper presents _Spacetime Dialogue_, a Space Art installation that explores the evolving relationship between humanity and the cosmos through a dialogue between contemporary astronomical observation and ancient nomadic cosmologies. The project establishes an artistic conversation between sonified astronomical data from the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) and Khoomei throat singing. Through processes of sonification, visualization, three-dimensional modeling, and digital fabrication, invisible cosmic signals and human vocal responses are transformed into tangible spatial structures. By integrating astronomy, anthropology, philosophy, acoustics, computer science, and traditional cultural practices, the work offers an embodied exploration of changing human–universe relationships across space and time.

# Contribution

- Proposes an interdisciplinary framework that transforms astronomical data and human vocal responses into immersive three-dimensional spatial structures, extending data visualization into material and experiential forms.
    
- Develops a dialogue model between JWST astronomical observations and Khoomei throat singing, connecting contemporary scientific observation with traditional nomadic cosmologies through sonification and spatial representation.
    
- Introduces a workflow that converts invisible and inaudible data into tangible sculptural installations through sound analysis, 3D modeling, and digital fabrication, enabling embodied experiences of human–universe relationships.
