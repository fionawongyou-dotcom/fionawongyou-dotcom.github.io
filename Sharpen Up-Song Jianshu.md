# Sharpen Up

## Exhibition Statement

*Sharpen Up* was the first solo sculpture exhibition by artist Song Jianshu, presenting a new phase in his artistic practice centered on the materiality of objects and the intrinsic qualities of matter itself.

Beginning in 2009, Song shifted his attention from representation toward a deeper investigation of material presence. Rather than imitating or reconstructing reality, he selectively intervenes in materials, allowing their physical properties to interact with personal perception and experience. His works emerge through a process in which material substance and subjective sensibility move toward one another, generating a dynamic state of collision and coexistence.

The exhibition title, *Sharpen Up* (剔), refers not only to subtraction and refinement but also to a method of construction through removal. Inspired by ideas similar to those articulated by Japanese Mono-ha artist Nobuo Sekine, Song seeks to reveal the existence of things as they are, allowing the essential nature of materials to become visible. Through acts of carving, reducing, and exposing, the artist establishes a connection between the origins of matter and individual spiritual experience. 

The exhibition unfolds as a journey through a series of sculptural forms arranged with a rhythmic and musical sensibility. Moving through the space, viewers encounter traces of an ongoing dialogue between physical substance and inner consciousness, witnessing the gradual convergence of material reality and spiritual reflection.
