# Ritualized Reset

Fiona You WANG

# Artist

Liang Hongwei

# Exhibition Statement

Ritualized Reset is a solo exhibition by artist Liang Hongwei that examines the relationship between technological systems, behavioral repetition, and contemporary ritual practices.

The exhibition explores how modern individuals continuously navigate cycles of construction, disruption, maintenance, and renewal within increasingly mediated environments. Borrowing visual language from digital interfaces, electronic infrastructures, and industrial systems, the works investigate how technological processes have gradually replaced traditional rituals as frameworks for organizing daily life and social experience.

Through installations, objects, and spatial interventions, Liang Hongwei transforms familiar technological forms into symbolic structures. Mechanical repetition, system errors, maintenance procedures, and acts of resetting become metaphors for contemporary psychological and social conditions. The exhibition asks whether the repetitive operations embedded within digital culture have themselves become new forms of ritual.

Presented within The Cure Room, the project invites visitors to reflect on the hidden protocols governing everyday behavior and the possibility of renewal within highly structured technological environments. By reimagining technical operations as cultural rituals, Ritualized Reset reveals the fragile balance between control, uncertainty, collapse, and regeneration in contemporary life.

### Exhibition Context

Ritualized Reset was presented as part of The Cure Room's early experimental exhibition program. The project explored emerging relationships between contemporary art, technological systems, behavioral patterns, and psychological experience, contributing to the development of later curatorial investigations into subjectivity, healing environments, and digital culture.
