# Abstract

This paper presents a computational media art practice grounded in the classical Chinese ontological framework of Heaven, Earth, and Human. Centered on an architectural lightwell, the project transforms a functional public space into an organic structure of spatial care through real-time astronomical sonification. By transducing stellar sequences, solar dynamics, and environmental light conditions into an immersive auditory environment, the framework reconstructs the relationship between cosmic rhythms, architectural space, and embodied perception. Through a triadic methodology integrating celestial data, environmental sensing, and physiological resonance, the work explores how computational aesthetics can support well-being, contemplative awareness, and sensory restoration in high-pressure institutional environments.

# Contribution

- Proposes a Heaven–Earth–Human ontological framework for computational media art, translating classical Chinese cosmology into a contemporary methodology for healing environments and spatial care.
    
- Develops a real-time sonification system that integrates astronomical observation, solar azimuth tracking, environmental light sensing, and spatialized audio to transform architectural lightwells into immersive contemplative interfaces.
    
- Demonstrates the therapeutic potential of astronomical sonification through an empirical workshop with 137 participants, showing improvements in perceived relaxation, spatial awareness, and embodied connection with natural rhythms.
