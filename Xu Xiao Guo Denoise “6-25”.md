# Noise Reduction

## Exhibition Statement

*Noise Reduction* was conceived as an event-based collaborative artwork that treated **human relationships and collaboration itself as artistic material**. Rather than presenting a finished object, the project unfolded through a complex interaction among artist Xu Xiaoguo, sound practitioners, painters, invited participants, and audiences.

At the center of the exhibition was an experimental process in which sound vibrations influenced the production of paintings. Loudspeakers installed beneath the canvas transmitted varying frequencies and intensities of sound, creating a dynamic field of resistance between sonic forces and painterly gestures. The resulting works were not understood as sound art, painting, or performance alone, but as the residue of a larger social and collaborative system.

The project challenged conventional notions of artistic authorship and questioned the stability of established artistic structures. Through participation, negotiation, interruption, and shared agency, *Noise Reduction* transformed exhibition-making into an evolving social environment. The exhibition proposed that artistic production could emerge not from individual expression alone but from collective processes of friction, cooperation, and disagreement.

As Xu Xiaoguo noted, the work was not about using sound to create a painting, but about generating meaning through the tension between multiple participants and systems. The exhibition therefore functioned as an experimental platform for exploring alternative models of creation, authorship, and value production. 

---

## Curatorial Concept

Curated by Fiona You Wang, *Noise Reduction* extended the idea of "fission collaboration" (裂变合作), a model in which participants work from shared agreements while maintaining independent goals and perspectives.

The exhibition investigated how artistic value might be generated through temporary alliances, distributed authorship, and collaborative structures. By emphasizing process over object and relationships over individual ownership, the project proposed a more open and fluid understanding of contemporary artistic practice. 

---

## Participatory Components

### Event Painting

Paintings were produced through interactions between sound vibrations and painterly interventions. The artist controlled the intensity and rhythm of speakers placed beneath the canvas, while painters responded to the shifting physical conditions generated by sound. 

### VIP Co-Creation Program

Selected participants were invited to become contractual collaborators in the production process. Through direct involvement in applying paint, they temporarily assumed the role of co-creators, challenging traditional distinctions between artist and audience. 

### Paint Theft Project

Visitors were encouraged to remove fragments of paint from designated works. This gesture transformed ownership, reproduction, and participation into active components of the exhibition, extending the artwork beyond the gallery space. 

### T-Shirt Intervention

Audience members could purchase and wear specially produced T-shirts that functioned simultaneously as tickets, documentation, and participatory artworks. During the final "explosion" phase of the event, paint traces accumulated on the garments, allowing visitors to physically carry fragments of the exhibition into everyday life.
