# Diffraction Field

# Exhibition Statement

_Diffraction Field_ is the first large-scale media arts festival organized by the Computational Media and Arts (CMA) Thrust at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou). Conceived as a transdisciplinary platform for artistic research, the project brings together exhibitions, performances, workshops, academic posters, and public symposia to explore the evolving relationships among computation, perception, materiality, and intelligence.

The concept of diffraction serves as both a scientific phenomenon and a curatorial framework. Beyond its physical meaning, diffraction becomes a metaphor for understanding the contemporary world, where boundaries between biological life and digital systems, physical matter and virtual environments, human cognition and machine intelligence continuously intersect and transform. Within this field of interactions, knowledge is not fixed but constantly produced through encounters among different disciplines, technologies, and ways of knowing.

Positioned within a technological ecosystem, CMA approaches media art as a dynamic site of negotiation between art, computer science, engineering, design, and biotechnology. The exhibition presents nearly thirty research-based artistic projects exploring machine vision, generative artificial intelligence, robotics, game engines, virtual reality, motion capture, sensing technologies, and computational environments. Together, these works question how perception, memory, embodiment, and materiality are being reconfigured in an age increasingly shaped by data and algorithmic systems.

Rather than treating media art as a discrete discipline, _Diffraction Field_ proposes it as an open and unstable space of experimentation. Through artistic practice, critical inquiry, and technological imagination, the exhibition invites audiences to explore new ways of seeing, sensing, and understanding a rapidly transforming world.
