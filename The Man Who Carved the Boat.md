# The Man Who Carved the Boat

# Exhibition Statement

*The Man Who Carved the Boat* is a comprehensive solo exhibition by Chinese contemporary artist Jiang Zhi. Taking its title from the ancient Chinese idiom *Ke Zhou Qiu Jian* (刻舟求剑, “carving a mark on a boat to search for a lost sword”), the exhibition reflects on the relationship between memory, change, time, and human attempts to anchor meaning within an ever-shifting world.

Bringing together works spanning more than two decades of artistic practice, the exhibition examines Jiang Zhi’s sustained engagement with questions of existence, perception, desire, love, uncertainty, and transformation. Working across photography, video, painting, sculpture, installation, literature, and moving image, Jiang continuously traverses the boundaries between personal experience and collective reality, poetic reflection and social observation.

Rather than presenting a chronological retrospective, *The Man Who Carved the Boat* unfolds as a philosophical journey through recurring themes that have shaped the artist's practice. The exhibition explores how individuals seek permanence within unstable circumstances and how memory itself becomes a creative act of reconstruction. The metaphor of carving a mark on a moving boat becomes a reflection on contemporary existence: a condition in which certainty constantly slips away while human beings continue searching for orientation and meaning.

Throughout the exhibition, Jiang Zhi approaches contemporary life through a distinctive poetic lens. Rather than offering fixed answers, his works reveal moments of fragility, tenderness, impermanence, and resilience, inviting viewers to reconsider their relationship with time, history, and lived experience.
