# Generative Artistic Affective Mandala
![](https://p.ipic.vip/is0hi2.png)

# Abstract

This paper presents a generative framework that transforms multimodal physiological signals into Kandinsky-inspired affective mandalas through a dual-stage synthesis integrating computational algorithms and latent diffusion models. Moving beyond conventional affective computing approaches that rely on emotional classification and numerical feedback, the framework translates continuous physiological dynamics—including electrodermal activity (EDA), heart rate variability (HRV), blood oxygen saturation (SpO₂), and inertial movement data (IMU)—into an interpretable visual grammar. By combining Jungian mandala structures, Kandinsky’s theories of form and color, and AI-driven image generation, the system externalizes physiological states into aesthetic artifacts that support self-reflection, affective literacy, and embodied interpretation. The research proposes a phenomenological alternative to diagnostic interfaces, positioning generative art as a medium for self-awareness and digital wellbeing.

# Contribution

- Proposes a systematic translation framework that maps multimodal physiological signals (EDA, HRV, SpO₂, IMU) into Kandinsky-inspired visual grammars, addressing a longstanding gap between biometric sensing and abstract artistic representation.
    
- Develops a hybrid generative architecture combining deterministic geometric constraints derived from Jungian mandala structures with latent diffusion models, improving interpretability and controllability in AI-generated affective visualization.
    
- Demonstrates that generative artistic feedback can function as an aesthetic mirror rather than a diagnostic report, fostering self-reflection, affective literacy, and interpretative agency through embodied interaction with physiological data.
