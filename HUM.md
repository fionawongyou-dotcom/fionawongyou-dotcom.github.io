# HUM

# Artwork Statement

_HUM_ is a sound installation that explores resonance between humanity and the cosmos through the convergence of astronomical data and Mongolian throat singing. Inspired by the transition from steppe nomads to cosmic nomads, the work reflects on how contemporary humanity continues to seek relationships with the universe through both cultural memory and technological mediation.

Drawing upon the Mongolian cosmological concept of Tengri—the eternal heaven and cosmos—the installation establishes a sonic dialogue between human vocal expression and astronomical signals. Sounds derived from celestial objects are transmitted through a distributed network of suspended three-dimensional structures, while throat singing introduces embodied cultural memory and nomadic cosmology into the system. Rather than representing outer space visually, the work creates an acoustic topology of relationships among humans, technology, and the cosmos.

The installation employs astronomical sonification, vibrating speakers, and custom-designed three-dimensional forms inspired by both cosmic molecular structures and human vocal organs. Audiences move through a field of resonant sound in which cosmic observation and human expression become intertwined. Through this experience, _HUM_ proposes sound as a medium for reimagining cosmological awareness beyond visual representation and anthropocentric perspectives.
