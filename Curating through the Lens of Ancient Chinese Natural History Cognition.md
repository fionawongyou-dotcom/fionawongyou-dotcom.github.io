# Curating through the Lens of Ancient Chinese Natural History Cognition

# Abstract

This paper introduces a transformative curatorial model inspired by the principles of Ancient Chinese Natural History, emphasizing the interconnectedness of life, harmony between humans and nature, and the universal life force underlying artistic creation. Through interdisciplinary and immersive curatorial methods, the study proposes a framework that bridges nature, technology, and humanity. Drawing from traditional Chinese cognitive approaches such as analogy, association, embodiment, and experiential knowledge, the paper demonstrates how curatorial practice can foster ecological awareness, subjective engagement, and holistic understanding. Through analyses of international exhibitions and the author's curatorial project Dream, Monolith and Revelation, the research explores new possibilities for integrating historical wisdom into contemporary curatorial innovation.

# Contribution

- Proposes a curatorial cognition framework grounded in Ancient Chinese Natural History and the principle of harmony between humans and nature.
    
- Introduces analogy, association, embodied knowledge, and subjective experience as methodological foundations for contemporary curatorial practice.
    
- Demonstrates how interdisciplinary, cross-cultural, and cross-temporal curatorial strategies can strengthen audience engagement and ecological awareness.
    
- Presents Dream, Monolith and Revelation as an experimental curatorial case study integrating Space Art, historical imagination, and personal narrative.
