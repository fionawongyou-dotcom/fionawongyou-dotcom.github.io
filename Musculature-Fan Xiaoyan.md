# Musculature

# Exhibition Statement

*Musculature* is a solo exhibition by Chinese artist Fan Xiaoyan that explores the subtle tensions between emotional experience, bodily perception, domestic space, and contemporary subjectivity. Through installation, object arrangements, and spatial interventions, the exhibition examines how invisible psychological structures become embedded within everyday life.

The title *Musculature* refers not only to the physical structure of the body, but also to the hidden systems of connection, tension, support, and vulnerability that shape contemporary existence. In Fan Xiaoyan's work, ordinary objects are transformed into carriers of emotional and social meanings. Familiar domestic materials are suspended, displaced, reorganized, and reimagined, creating delicate relationships between gravity and resistance, order and instability, intimacy and estrangement.

Presented within The Cure Room, an experimental space dedicated to art, healing, and embodied reflection, the exhibition invites viewers to enter a contemplative environment where perception unfolds slowly. Rather than offering direct narratives, the works generate subtle encounters between body, memory, emotion, and space.

Through these suspended structures and carefully composed environments, *Musculature* explores how contemporary individuals navigate invisible forces within everyday life. The exhibition suggests that emotional experience, like musculature itself, is simultaneously fragile and resilient, hidden yet essential to our capacity for connection and movement.
