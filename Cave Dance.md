# Cave Dance 起舞敦煌

# Exhibition Statement

Following the archival exhibition _From the Lo Archive to Visioning Dunhuang_, _Cave Dance_ extends the conversation from preservation to activation, asking a new question: what happens when the celestial dancers of Dunhuang leave the cave wall and enter the realm of data?

Developed by the Computational Media and Arts (CMA) team at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (Guangzhou) in collaboration with Harvard University's Chinese Art Media Lab (CAMLab), the exhibition presents _Chang-E_, the world's first open-source comprehensive motion-capture dataset for Dunhuang dance. Through high-precision motion capture technologies, forty minutes of professional Dunhuang dance across eight movement categories were transformed into computable digital skeletons and body models, creating a new foundation for the preservation, study, and creative reinterpretation of embodied cultural heritage.

Rather than treating Dunhuang as a static historical object, the exhibition approaches it as a living archive capable of continuous transformation. Using AI-based motion inbetweening algorithms, retiming technologies, and immersive digital visualization, the project enables ancient movements to unfold within virtual environments beyond the constraints of gravity, materiality, and historical reconstruction. In this process, the exhibition explores how traditional concepts of bodily cognition can be re-examined through computational media.

At the center of the exhibition is _Cave Screen_, an immersive digital installation that transforms visual elements, movement vocabularies, colors, and compositional structures derived from Dunhuang murals into a contemporary spatial experience. Entering the installation, visitors encounter a digitally constructed cave where historical imagery, motion data, and computational aesthetics converge, creating a new encounter between cultural heritage and emerging technologies.

_Cave Dance_ proposes that preservation is only the beginning of cultural transmission. Through the integration of digital humanities, motion capture, artificial intelligence, and immersive media, the exhibition demonstrates how heritage can continue to generate new meanings, new experiences, and new forms of creative practice in the contemporary world.
