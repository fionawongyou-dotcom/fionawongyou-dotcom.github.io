# Quantum-Inspired Images as Epistemic Art: A Tripartite Framework of Semiotics, Phenomenology, and Sociotechnical Systems
![Transport VI, 2001, an artwork image by Eric J. Heller](https://p.ipic.vip/g6cvgz.jpeg)

# Abstract

This paper examines quantum-inspired images as a distinct artistic genre emerging at the intersection of quantum physics, scientific visualization, and contemporary art. Arguing that traditional aesthetic theories are insufficient for interpreting artworks rooted in scientific complexity, the study proposes an interdisciplinary framework integrating Charles Sanders Peirce’s semiotics, Maurice Merleau-Ponty’s phenomenology, and Niklas Luhmann’s sociotechnical systems theory. Through analyses of representative quantum-inspired artworks, the research demonstrates how these images function simultaneously as aesthetic objects, epistemic tools, and science communication media. The framework reveals how quantum-inspired images translate abstract scientific concepts into accessible visual experiences while fostering public engagement with quantum theory and contemporary technological culture.

# Contribution

- Proposes a tripartite theoretical framework combining semiotics, phenomenology, and sociotechnical systems theory to evaluate quantum-inspired images, addressing limitations of conventional art criticism when applied to science-based artistic practices.
    
- Demonstrates how quantum-inspired images function as epistemic artifacts that mediate between specialized scientific knowledge and public understanding through layered processes of interpretation, perception, and meaning-making.
    
- Establishes quantum-inspired art as a sociotechnical practice situated within collaborative networks of artists, scientists, institutions, and audiences, extending discussions of Public Understanding of Science through artistic visualization.
